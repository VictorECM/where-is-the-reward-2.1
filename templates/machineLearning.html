Clustering Analysis

 

Currently, the company has grouped its clients in 6 categories, the objective of this analysis is to determine if it is necessary to modify them.

For the present analysis, unsupervised "K-Means" method was used. This algorithm seeks to cluster data by their related characteristics.

To determine of the optimal number of clusters the Elbow Method was used.

As a general premise, itÂ´s evident that customers with few sales are very costly in points. The objective of the program should be that with more sales, more points they should gain to get a fair reward for sales:

 

Total Sales vs Average Sales per Month

The first exercise is defining the groups based on the sales each Customer has had, viewed from 2 approaches, the total sales: from 2018 to 2020,
and the average sales amount per month as there are new customers who have fewer months in the program, and it would be "unfair" to compare it to a customer who has been permanent in the scheme.

The optimal number of clusters with these two variables is set to 3 by the elbow method.

 

 

In conclusion, there is no obvious clustering of customers by distributor,customers could be reorganized and the definition of categories can be simplified from 6 to 3.

Group 1: Customers with average monthly sales greater than 250 thousand pesos.

Group 2: Customers with average monthly sales of less than 250 thousand pesos and accumulated sales in the program of more than 2.5 million pesos.

Group 3: Customers with average monthly sales of less than 250 thousand pesos and accumulated sales in the program of less than 2.5 million pesos.
